Croatia Defeat Egypt 4-2 in Cairo Friendly With Goals From Vlašić, Petković, Kramarić & Majer

March 25, 2024 By Ante Kvartuč 87 Comments

FINAL: The boys woke up from an anemic offensive display against Tunisia on Saturday with 4 goals from Vlašić, Petković, Kramarić and Majer. The attack looked much more lively in the 1st half as Kovačić started the match on the bench in lieu of Majer.Both Egypt goals were scored from set pieces.

87′: GOOOOOALLLL!! Kramarić lays the ball to Majer at the top of the box. Majer takes a shot that looks to take a deflection but it hit the back of the net nonetheless. 4-1 Croatia!

78′” GOOOOOAL!! Majer with the free kick from the middle of the field and Kramarić heads home from an extremely tight angle. 3-1 Croatia!

72′: Croatia with some tiki-tala ball all the way up field. Mario Pašalić chests the ball down in the box and rifles the shot over the bar. Could have been #3.

58′: GOALLLLLLL Croatia! Bruno Petković is left absolutely wide open on a corner kick and cooly heads it home with all the time in the world. 2-1 Croatia.

Halftime subs. Kotarski comes in for Labrović in goal. Kovačić subbed in for Brozović.

HT: Croatia looks so much better offensively without Kova clogging up the midfield doing nothing. Pjaca looked great and should have had an assist. Majer and Vlašić both looking lively. That 1st half is Ante Kvartuč approved!

45′: Pjaca puts a perfect ball to Petko in front of the goal with no goalie in sight. Petko misses the sitter. It was actually harder to miss than to score. Where is Budimir?

41′: Croatia peppering the Egyptian goal with chances. Looking good. Another corner for the good guys.

38′: Vida nearly scores! Majer whips in a free kick that bounces across the box. Vida dives for the ball and misses the free header by inches!

34′: Majer takes his defender 1:1 and puts a fine shot on goal. It looks good but the deflection causes a corner.

25′: I know it’s just a friendly in March and I will be the first person to tell you these results mean nothing. However, the team looks WAY BETTER with Majer/Vlašić in the midfield compared to Kovačić/Pašalić. Much more speed. #justsayin

20′: GOOOOOOALLL Nikola Vlašić!!! What a strike! Vlašić dribbles across the 18-yard line and then left foots a perfect shot over the goalie to kiss the bottom of the cross bar to cross the goal line.

18′: Lots of action! Petko loops the ball to Vlašić on the back post but the Torino man can’t get a solid shot on the ball. Should have been a corner.

16′: Egypt quickly counter and the 1:1 results in a shot off the left post. Should easily be 2-0 Egypt.

15′: Croatia peppering the Egypt goal with 2 crosses. A header (from Petko?) almost beat the Egyptian goalie.

7′: The goal is good. No player was offside after the VAR review. 1-0 Egypt. Set pieces once again the Achilles Heel of the Croatian defense.

6′: HOLD ON! A long VAR review is looking at the goal.

5′: Egypt score following a free kick from the corner of the box. Labrović stops the initial shot on goal but doesn’t parry the ball far enough away. Egypt puts the ball back in the box and the cross is connected on and in the back of the net.

3′: Croatia on the front foot out of the gate. Already a better paced start compared to Tunisia Saturday.

1′: What a beautiful, brand new stadium in Cairo! It’s packed with what looks like 70,000 Egyptian supporters with a sprinkle of checkers. This os only the second match ever to be played in this stadium.

The match can be streamed free on FIFA+ with an email/password.

Croatia Starting XI vs. Egypt: Labrović – Stanišić, Vida, Pongračić, Gvardiol – Modrić, Brozović, Majer – Vlašić, Pjaca – Petković

Per Sportske Novosti, there will be tons of changes for Tuesday night’s clash (4 PM ET) between Croatia and Egypt in Cairo.

Dalić announced several changes in the lineup, saying that defender Martin Erlić and left winger Luka Ivanušec will not play against Egypt due to minor injuries. Erlić picked up a knock against Tunisia and Ivanušec limped off the field with a small hamstring tweak.

Dalić commented, “We have 17 players available which will be enough. We will use all six substitutes at our disposal. Labrović and Kotarski will play a half each in goal.” That means Livaković and Ivušić (the MOTM vs. Tunisia for his penalty kick shootout win) will not be featured. 

Dalić wasn’t quite happy with the Croatia attack vs. Tunisia. “We created too few chances and had too few shots on goal. That was our handicap. We will have better going forward.”

I imagine with all that said we will see plenty of Majer in the center of the field. I’m also excited to see how much time Rijeka’s Marko Pjaca and Marco Pašalic with get on the wings. That is Croatia’s Achilles Heel at the moment: wings.
